History of The UAE

The United Arab Emirates (UAE) is a young nation with a rich history rooted in ancient traditions and maritime trade. For centuries, the region was inhabited by nomadic tribes and served as a hub for pearl diving, fishing, and trade along the Arabian Gulf. In the early 20th century, the pearling industry declined, and the region shifted its focus to oil exploration. On December 2, 1971, six emirates united to form the UAE, with Ras Al Khaimah joining shortly after in 1972. Under the visionary leadership of Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan, the UAE transformed from a desert landscape into a global hub for trade, tourism, and innovation. Today, it is celebrated for its rapid development, cultural heritage, and modernity.
